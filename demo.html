Writing & Thoughts

Frontend

Why I care about micro-interactions

How small animations and thoughtful transitions transform a good interface into a memorable one...

Micro-interactions are the subtle animations and feedback loops that happen when a user engages with an interface. A button that depresses slightly, a form field that glows on focus, or a card that lifts on hover — these details communicate state, provide confirmation, and create delight. I believe they are what separates a functional interface from a polished product. They require attention to timing, easing, and the psychology of feedback.

Design Systems

Building scalable CSS without frameworks

Lessons learned from maintaining large vanilla CSS codebases with custom properties and modular architecture...

Frameworks are useful, but they come with opinions that may not match your project. Vanilla CSS with custom properties gives you the same power of theming and consistency without the bundle overhead. The key is establishing a clear naming convention, defining a finite set of design tokens, and organizing styles by component rather than by page. Over time, this approach scales better because you own every rule.

Career

Learning in public as a second-year student

Why documenting your journey is one of the best investments you can make in your growth as a developer...

Learning in public means sharing what you build, what breaks, and what you figure out along the way. For me, it started with posting small projects on GitHub and writing short notes about solutions I found. The benefits compound: you reinforce your own understanding, you build a portfolio of progress, and you attract mentors and collaborators who resonate with your trajectory. It is not about being an expert — it is about being curious and consistent.
